a response to Ursula K. Le Guin's, A Rant on Technology

things i found interesting:


you can't really describe a space in time without mentioning technology in some form; whether that’s how advanced it has become, or the lack thereof


“technology is how society copes with physical reality” or how efficiently we can complete the tasks we set out to do


something being “hi” or “low” tech is entirely subjective; those who discovered “low” tech probably thought it was “hi” at the moment of discovery
